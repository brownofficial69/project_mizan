Project MIZAN

A wall the optimiser cannot cross

An interactive demonstration of two mechanisms: an interior-point barrier that holds a hard constraint boundary, and a monitoring gate that computes a safety margin. The maths below runs live in your browser.

Concept demo. Not a real model. Telemetry is synthetic and labelled. CC0 1.0 Universal.

Mechanism 1

The optimisation wall

The task pulls the parameter toward theta = 5. A hard constraint forbids theta above 3. A log-barrier keeps the parameter strictly on the safe side. Raise the barrier strength and watch it settle against the wall without ever passing it.

Mechanism 2

The safety gate

The gate reads a state, computes an alignment value, its spread, an entropy, and a robustness estimate, then combines them into a margin. If the margin drops below zero, the gate trips. The state here is synthetic: it does not read a model and does not understand text.

What this is: a correct, self-contained demonstration of the barrier and the gate. What it is not: a working alignment system, a real model, or an AGI. The barrier is a standard interior-point method. The gate is not calibrated, and its thresholds and alignment read-out are placeholders.
